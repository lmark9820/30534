Museum Tour

The guide tour in the National Museum of Natural Science

About Us

Chung Ming Senior High School is close to the National Museum of Natural Science.Because of the convenience of distance, our school’s mandatory course uses the exhibitions in the science museum as the class content, leading us to learn seven of the exhibitions and derived content in English. In this class, I learned many things that I didn't pay attention to before, such as the Mikado Pheasant, which is endemic to Taiwan, the caisson ceiling in Wan Fu Kon , the fossil of Penghu Ancient elephants. Furthermore, I acquired valuable skills like making brochures and introducing different exhibits. I also collaborated with my team members to film a video showcasing the Mikado Pheasant.

The funniest and most difficult things to do in class.

There are many unforgettable things in this class. I did a lot of things that I couldn't believe I could do.Like building a small slate house with slate and white glue, and I, who don't like the smell on my hands, even touched octopus and squid for a whole class. The funniest thing is building the slate house. In our happy and even crazy atmosphere (Building a slate house is hard without going crazy.), from the first few pieces of wood and a bag of slate to the last small memorial slate house, we built it step by step with hands full of white glue.Shooting the film proves to be the most challenging aspect. In addition to the tasks of writing, memorizing, and recording, we had the unfortunate timing of coinciding with the exhibition's maintenance period. Fortunately, the museum houses an additional specimen of Mikado Pheasant.



Besides course activities, the exhibits I am most interested in.

In addition to the seven exhibits we introduced in class, there is another exhibit that I am very interested in in the National Museum of Natural Science, and that is dinosaurs. There is an animated model of Tyrannosaurus rex four meters high and seven meters long in the Life Science Museum. Since the renovation of the Dinosaur Hall was completed in August 2004, it has become a must-see exhibit for many people who come to the Science and Technology Museum, and it is still very popular. At Christmas, the staff of the Science Museum also helped the dinosaurs put on Christmas costumes, which is super cute!
