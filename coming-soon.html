Wan Fu Kon

In the museum, Wan Fu Kon is an exhibit rich in Chinese elements.I will introduce three interesting elements. First, when you visit the exhibits, you will see three doors. The door on your right when you face the temple is the dragon door, it is for people to enter the temple. The door in the middle is for the gods. The door at the left is the tiger door, it is the exit of the temple. In Chinese culture,it is considered auspicious to enter a temple with the dragon and exit with the tiger. Second, In the Wan Fu kon, you can see many dragon carvings. In Chinese culture, dragons symbolize power, nobility, and honor, and are also a symbol of auspiciousness and success.It is said that the dragon has the eyes of a cat, allowing it to see things in the dark. In addition, the dragon has the sharp claws of an eagle.Finally, there is another common element found in temples, which is the decoration of the roof. Common ones are the three gods representing wealth, fame, and longevity, the fish that symbolize fire prevention, and carved pagodas, the higher the floor of the pagoda, the higher the god of the main temple in the bureaucracy of the gods.
