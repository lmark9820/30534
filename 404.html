Elephant

On the first floor of the Life Science Hall of the science museum, there is a specimen of an ancient elephant, which was found in the Penghu Channel.With the specimen of this ancient elephant, we learned about elephants. Elephants are the heaviest animals on land . Existing elephants can be divided into African elephants and Asian elephants. We can easily distinguish them, for example, Asian elephants are smaller in size and ears than African elephants.Their trunks have many functions and can be used to smell, breathe, and grab objects. In particular, the elephant trunk contains about 100,000 different muscles.In addition, Asian elephants have a fingerlike feature on the end of their trunk that they can use to grab small items.(African elephants have two.)Their tusks also have many functions, they can be used for digging roots, digging water, stripping bark from trees and fighting. It is a pity that many elephants are killed for their ivory because of people's desire. Although the trade of ivory is now illegal, it cannot be completely eliminated.
