Paiwan Slate Houses

Paiwan Slate House is a traditional architecture built by the Paiwan tribe. The slate house is mainly made out of slate slabs and woods.There are also male and female stones in the Paiwan people.Harder and denser slates are referred to as "male stone", which suitable for building houses.Brittle ones as"female stone" that they are good for building small walls or stairways. There are also some wooden or stone decorations around the house, like the hundred pacer snake designs. The carving of the hundred step snake can be seen in the leader's home.In addition Main Pillar and Ancestral Pillar can be seen in the house.They not only can support weight of the roof, but also symbol of the presence of ancestral spirit.There are skylights on the roof , so wind and light can get in the house, and also has a defensive function. When the enemy attacks, the tribe can use the location of the skylight to fight back.
