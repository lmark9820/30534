Giant Squid

The giant squid display,which is the largest vertically presented squid in the world, is situated on the first floor of the Global Environment Hall. It was captured off of the coast of New Zealand and donated to the museum by researchers from the New Zealand National Institute of Water & Atmospheric Research.Through this exhibition, we learn about the related creatures of cephalopods.
Among the cephalopods I would like to introduce are squid and octopus. The first creature I want to introduce is the squid, which has a triangular-shaped head and ten arms with suction rings. Moreover, it has a small soft shell inside its body. Squid live in the deep sea and use their fins to move slowly (Although they can also move very fast by propelling themselves with water, but this is very energy-intensive.) They can defend themselves by spraying ink.
Next, we have the octopus. It has a round head and eight arms, but it does not have suction rings. Unlike squid, it is completely soft. Octopuses live on the ocean floor and walk or crawl along it. They are masters of camouflage and rely on this ability to hide from enemies.
